# Executive Readout Template

The Executive Readout translates ContextBoundary findings into business risk, investment priorities, and accountable next steps.

Use this template after discovery, assessment, scoring, and contract review are complete.

## 1. Executive Summary

Answer three questions:

- Where is AI data allowed to go today?
- Where is it actually going?
- What decisions are required to close the gap?

## 2. Current-State Boundary Snapshot

Include:

- AI workloads reviewed
- Boundary Zones in use
- vendors in scope
- jurisdictions in scope
- Audit Profiles considered
- known MCP servers, tools, APIs, and context sources

## 4. Top Risks

List the top five risks in business language:

- silent egress
- over-exposed MCP tools
- Tier I data crossing without transformation
- vendor contract mismatch
- missing audit evidence
- telemetry leakage
- subprocessor opacity

## 5. Business Impact

Frame impact in terms executives can act on:

- regulatory exposure
- vendor lock-in
- procurement risk
- data sovereignty risk
- delayed AI adoption
- incident response weakness
- inability to prove control during audit

## 6. Remediation Roadmap

Use a 30/60/90-day plan:

| Timeframe | Remediation Theme | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 30 days | Boundary visibility | AI topology map, crossing inventory, high-risk containment |
| 60 days | Control implementation | Egress Tier policy, MCP filtering, approval gates, logging path |
| 90 days | Operating model | stakeholder approval cadence, contract remediation, drift monitoring |

## 7. Decisions Required

Name the decisions executives must make:

- Which AI workloads are in scope first?
- Which Tier I data must remain inside the Customer Sovereign Zone?
- Which vendors require contract remediation?
- Which agent actions require human approval?
- Which audit evidence must be retained?
- Who owns the boundary after the consulting engagement ends?

## 8. Next Step

Recommend a concrete next action:

```text
Approve a 30-day ContextBoundary remediation sprint for the highest-risk workload.
```
